How Much Does EV Charger Installation Cost?

Installing a Level 2 EV charger at home typically costs between $500 and $2,500, depending on your electrical panel capacity, wiring distance, and local labor rates. In California, costs run higher due to permitting requirements, while Texas installations tend to be more affordable. Use our EV cost calculator for a personalized estimate based on your specific location.

Most homeowners qualify for a federal tax credit of 30% (up to $1,000) through the Inflation Reduction Act, plus additional state-level rebates. See our comprehensive rebate and tax credit guide for all available incentives in your state.

What Type of EV Charger Should You Get?

For most homeowners, a Level 2 charger (240V) is the right choice. It adds 25-35 miles of range per hour and fully charges most EVs in 4-8 hours. Popular brands include Tesla Wall Connector, ChargePoint Home Flex, JuiceBox 40, and Emporia. Take our charger quiz to find the perfect match for your vehicle and budget, or use our side-by-side comparison tool.
