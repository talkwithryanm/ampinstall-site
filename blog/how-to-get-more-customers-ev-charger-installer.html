2. Optimize Your Google Business Profile

This is the single highest-ROI thing you can do. Google Business Profile drives local search results. If yours isn't optimized, you're leaving money on the table.

Must-haves:

  • Complete every field (hours, phone, website, services)
  • Add EV charger installation as a service category
  • Upload 10+ photos of your work (installed chargers, your truck, your team)
  • Respond to every review — good and bad
  • Post updates weekly (specials, recent installs, tips)

Installers who fully complete their Google profile get 7x more clicks than those with partial profiles.

3. Get Reviews (The Right Way)

Reviews are the #1 factor homeowners use to choose an installer. But asking every customer for a review feels awkward.

The system that works:

  1. Finish the job. Customer is happy.
  2. Send a follow-up text within 2 hours: "Glad we got your charger installed. Would you mind leaving a quick review? Here's the link."
  3. Respond to every review within 24 hours. Thank them. If it's negative, apologize and offer to make it right.

Pro tip: Create a QR code that links directly to your Google review page. Print it on your invoice.

4. Specialize in EV Chargers

Most electricians do everything. That makes you a generalist. Homeowners searching for EV charger installation don't want a generalist — they want someone who specializes.

  • Mention EV charger installation on your website homepage
  • Create a dedicated page for Tesla Wall Connector or ChargePoint installation
  • Get certified with major brands (Tesla, ChargePoint, JuiceBox)
  • Showcase 5+ charger install photos on your site and Google profile

5. Partner with Local EV Dealerships

Tesla, Ford, Chevy, and Hyundai dealers sell EVs every day. Every customer needs a charger installed. Most dealers don't have a go-to installer.

The pitch: Walk into the service department. Ask for the manager. Say: "I'm a licensed EV charger installer. I'll give your customers a 10% discount. You send them my way."

6. Join Nextdoor and Facebook Groups

Homeowners ask for recommendations constantly. If you're not there, you're invisible. Join local groups. Don't spam. Be helpful. Answer questions. When someone asks for a recommendation, respond naturally.

This single tactic brings in 3-5 leads per month. Free.
