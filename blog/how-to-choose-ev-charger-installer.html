How to Choose an EV Charger Installer

📅 January 22, 2026

Not every electrician is ready to install an EV charger. The difference is usually not whether they can wire a 240V circuit — it is whether they understand load calculations, EV-specific hardware, permit requirements, and manufacturer warranty conditions.

This guide covers what to look for, what to ask, and what signals it is time to keep looking.

By EV Charger Installer Near Me. Last updated May 2026.

Signals a cheaper installer might cost you more

  • No mention of load calculation — guessing instead of measuring your panel capacity
  • Can't name the EV charger brands they install regularly
  • Doesn't mention permits or says "you can pull the permit yourself"
  • Cash-only or no written quote
  • No liability insurance or license number on the quote
  • Quotes a flat price without seeing your panel or run path

Better questions to ask

  • Do you perform a load calculation before quoting? — The answer should be yes.
  • Which charger brands have you installed most often? — Should be at least 3 brands.
  • Will you handle the permit? — Yes. A licensed installer should include this.
  • What is your license number? — Verify it with your state licensing board.
  • Do you warranty your work? — Standard is 1 year minimum on labor.

Direct answers

What certification should an EV charger installer have?

The EVITP (Electric Vehicle Infrastructure Training Program) certification is the most recognized credential. It covers load calculations, charger-specific installation, permit requirements, and safety. Not all states require it, but it is a strong signal of expertise.

Can any electrician install an EV charger?

Any licensed electrician can install a 240V circuit, but EV charger installation requires understanding load management, EV-specific hardware, and manufacturer compatibility. Experience with EV chargers specifically reduces mistakes.

Should I get multiple quotes?

Yes. Get at least 3 quotes. The cheapest quote that includes load calculation, permit handling, and a written warranty is often the best value — not the lowest number.

What still matters outside the quote

Installation quality affects charger performance, safety, and warranty coverage. A bad installation can damage your charger, fail inspection, or create fire risk. Always verify license and insurance before hiring. Browse verified installers.

EV charger installer FAQs

How do I know if an installer is licensed?

Ask for their license number and look it up on your state's licensing board website. Most states have online verification tools.

What happens if the installation fails inspection?

A licensed installer should return and correct the issue at no charge. This is why a written warranty matters.

Do all installers offer the same brands?

No. Some installers specialize in Tesla Wall Connectors, others work with ChargePoint, JuiceBox, or Wallbox. Ask before booking.
